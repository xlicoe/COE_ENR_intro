Why earn your B.S. in Engineering at Coe?

B.S. in Engineering Physics: Starting 2024-25, this degree is built upon Coe’s internationally well-regarded physics program (already the largest physics programs in Iowa).

Interdisciplinary Core: Merges liberal arts tradition with rigorous engineering, sharpening creative problem-solving skills in high demand.

Job Market Outlook: Employment in engineering fields is projected to grow faster than average over the next decade. Engineering wages are expected to exceed national averages by nearly $40,000 annually.

Most Accessible Faculty: Ranked No. 1 in Iowa and No. 7 Nationally for professor accessibility by The Princeton Review.

Launchpad for Success

Experiential Guarantee: All Coe students are guaranteed an internship, research project, or off-campus study before graduation. Coe is No. 12 nationally for internships.

Elite Placements: Kohawks conduct research at CERN and attend grad programs at Oxford, MIT, and Northwestern.

Career Outcomes: For the past decade, nearly 100% of reporting Coe graduates have been employed or in graduate school within nine months of graduation.

Top Partners: Internship at NASA, Google, Collins Aerospace, and Corning, Inc.
